Köttbullar in cranberry sauce

A swedish classic!

Köttbullar med gräddsås (meatballs with cream sauce) is widely thought of as Sweden's national dish.

Köttbullar are smaller and have a diferent flavour to Italian meatballs and are nearly always served with a cream sauce.

The recipe first appeared in a book by the legendary Cajsa Warg, published in 1755, and has been a firm favourite ever since.

INGREDIENTS

Köttbullar (meatballs)

  • White bread (approx. 50 g) - 1 slice
  • Milk, hot - 1/2 dl
  • Minced beef - 500g
  • Onion, finely chopped - 1
  • Flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped - 1/2 bunch
  • Salt - 3/4 tsp
  • Pepper - a little
  • Clarified butter for frying

Cranberry sauce

  • White wine - 1 dl
  • Meat bouillon - 1 dl
  • Crème fraîche - 2 tbsp
  • Cranberries from a jar - 1 tbsp
  • Salt and pepper to taste - 1/4 tsp
  • Cranberries from a jar, to garnish - 1 tbsp

HOW IT'S DONE

Köttbullar (meatballs)

  1. Soak the bread in the milk, tear apart, place in a bowl along with the meat, onion and parsley, season.
  2. Knead all the ingredients to form a compact mass that no longer sticks to your hands.
  3. With wet hands, shape into walnut-sized balls. Cover and refrigerate for approx. 10 mins.
  4. Fry the meatballs in a little clarified butter over a medium heat for approx. 8 mins. per batch, stirring occasionally. Remove from the pan.

Cranberry sauce

  1. Wipe the clarified butter from the frying pan.
  2. Pour in the wine and stock, bring to the boil, reduce to half the amount.
  3. Stir in the crème fraîche and cranberries, bring to the boil, reduce the heat, season.
  4. Return the meatballs to the pan, simmer over a low heat for approx. 5 mins.
  5. Garnish with cranberries before serving.
